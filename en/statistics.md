# Statistics and Writing Goals

Skribane tracks your writing activity to help you maintain a consistent pace and reach your goals.

## Accessing Statistics

You have two ways to view your statistics:

- **Right panel** (Statistics tab) — Compact summary always visible while writing.
- **Statistics mode** — Full view with chart and goal configuration (`Ctrl+3`).

## Writing Goals

You can configure the following goals from Statistics mode:

- **Total goal** — The word count you want your novel to reach (e.g., 80,000).
- **Daily goal** — Words per day to maintain your pace (e.g., 500).
- **Weekly goal** — Words per week.
- **Monthly goal** — Words per month.

Goals are saved per project: each novel can have its own objectives.

## Right Panel — Quick Summary

The Statistics tab in the right panel shows:

- **Daily goal** — Progress bar with today's completion percentage.
- **Streak** — Consecutive days you've written. Resets if a day passes without writing.
- **Activity** — Words written this week, this month, and daily average.
- **Total progress** — Progress bar toward your total goal, with an estimated completion date based on your current pace.

## Statistics Mode — Full View

Statistics mode (`Ctrl+3`) offers:

- **3 summary cards** — Words today, current streak, and best streak.
- **30-day chart** — Bars showing words written each day over the past month. Days where you met the daily goal are shown in a different color.
- **Configure goals** — Inputs to change the daily goal and total goal.
- **Projection** — Estimate of how many days remain to finish the novel and estimated date.

## Automatic Tracking

Skribane automatically records every word you write (or delete) while editing. No special action needed: simply write and statistics update in real time.

Tracking also counts AI-generated words that you accept, because they become part of your manuscript.
